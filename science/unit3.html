Your blood includes thirteen different protein molecules called clotting factors. When you injure yourself, these clotting factor proteins all connect and work together to form a solid clot. If you were missing even one of those thirteen proteins, the clot wouldnâ€™t form. People with hemophilia are missing one of the proteins needed for blood clotting. People with hemophilia are missing clotting factor proteins because of a problem with their genes. Different genes carry instructions for making different proteins.

Chapter 2:

We inherit our genes from our biological parents through the process of sexual reproduction. Each parent has a complete set of genes. These genes are organized on matching pairs of chromosomes and each has two copies of each gene.If a parent has two different versions of the gene, some of the reproductive cells will end up with one version, while other reproductive cells will end up with the other version.
